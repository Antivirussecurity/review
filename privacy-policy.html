Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to AntivirusReview

This Privacy Policy explains how AntivirusReview ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, stores and protects your information when you visit our website, read reviews, submit forms or interact with our content. Please read it carefully to understand what data we collect, why we collect it and the choices you have. If you do not agree with this policy, you should stop using our website. For any privacy-related questions, you can contact us at info@AntivirusReview.com.

1. Who We Are

AntivirusReview is an independent review and comparison platform focused on antivirus and cybersecurity products. We research, test and review software solutions to help users choose better digital protection for their devices. While doing so, we may process certain personal and technical information about you in line with this policy and applicable data protection laws.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information in the following ways:

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

This includes information you enter or submit on our site, such as:

  • Name and contact details (e.g., email address) when you contact us or subscribe to updates.
  • Information shared when you submit a blog, become a contributor or request collaboration.
  • Messages or details submitted through contact forms and support forms.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit AntivirusReview, some technical data is collected automatically to keep our site secure and running properly. This may include:

  • IP address, browser type, device type, operating system and approximate location (country/region).
  • Pages you visit, time spent on pages, links clicked and referring URLs.
  • Device identifiers and general interaction patterns for analytics and performance monitoring.

2.3 Cookies & Similar Technologies

We use cookies, pixels and similar tracking technologies to:

  • Remember your basic preferences (such as language or cookie choices).
  • Understand how visitors use our site so we can improve content and layout.
  • Show relevant content or advertising where applicable.

You can control or disable cookies through your browser settings. Some features of the site may not work properly if certain cookies are disabled.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for purposes such as:

  • Providing, maintaining and improving our website, content and services.
  • Responding to your queries, feedback, partnership or support requests.
  • Analyzing usage trends to improve user experience and site performance.
  • Sending you updates or promotional content if you have opted in (you can unsubscribe anytime).
  • Detecting, preventing and addressing technical, security or fraudulent activities.
  • Complying with legal obligations, resolving disputes and enforcing our terms.

4. Legal Bases for Processing (Where Applicable)

In regions like the EU, UK and Canada, we rely on one or more of the following legal bases to process your data:

  • Consent: When you voluntarily provide information, sign up or accept cookies.
  • Legitimate Interests: To run, protect and improve our site, prevent abuse and analyze usage.
  • Contractual Necessity: When we need data to respond to your requests or provide a service.
  • Legal Obligations: When we must keep or share data to comply with laws or regulatory requirements.

5. When We Share Your Information

We do not sell your personal information as a product. However, we may share certain data with:

  • Service providers who help us with hosting, analytics, email delivery, security and performance.
  • Advertising and affiliate partners to measure conversions, clicks and campaign effectiveness.

  • Business successors in case of a merger, acquisition or similar business transfer, where permitted by law.

All service providers are expected to protect your data and use it only for the specific purpose for which it is shared.

6. International Data Transfers

Our website may be accessed from anywhere in the world and some of our service providers or servers may be located outside your country (for example, in the United States or other regions). By using our website, you understand that your information may be transferred to and processed in countries that may have different data protection laws than your home country. Where required, we take reasonable steps to ensure appropriate safeguards are in place.

7. How Long We Keep Your Information

We keep your personal information only for as long as it is reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law (for example, tax or legal reasons). When data is no longer needed, we will delete it or anonymize it so it can no longer be linked back to you.

8. How We Protect Your Information

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to help protect your personal information from unauthorized access, loss, misuse, alteration or disclosure. However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. You should use strong passwords, keep your devices safe and avoid sharing sensitive information over unsecured networks.

9. Children’s Privacy

Our website and content are not directed at children under 18 years of age, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If we become aware that we have collected information from a child under 18, we will take reasonable steps to delete such data. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us at info@AntivirusReview.com.

Depending on your location and local laws (for example, in the EU, UK, EEA, Canada or certain US states), you may have some or all of the following rights:

  • Right to access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to request correction or update of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to request deletion of your personal data, subject to lawful exceptions.
  • Right to object to or restrict certain types of processing (such as direct marketing).
  • Right to withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.
  • Right to data portability in some circumstances.

To exercise any of these rights, you can contact us using the details provided in the How to Contact Us section below. We may need to verify your identity before responding to your request.

Some browsers also send a “Do Not Track” (DNT) signal — currently, there is no widely accepted standard for how websites should respond, so we do not guarantee specific behavior in response to DNT signals. You can still manage cookies directly in your browser settings.
